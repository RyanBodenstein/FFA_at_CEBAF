## Task 21c: Error Budget Comparison

### Sample scatter (std dev of individual measurements)

| Type | NdFeB std (%) | N | SmCo std (%) | N |
|------|--------------|---|-------------|---|
| Y-plate | 0.197 | 59 | 0.171 | 59 |
| H-plate | 0.307 | 37 | 0.424 | 48 |
| A-slot  | 0.154 | 13 | 0.136 | 16 |

H-plates are 1.6x noisier (NdFeB) and 2.5x noisier (SmCo) than Y-plates.
A-slots look less noisy but have far fewer points (N=13 vs N=59).

### Why combining Y+H+A dilutes the signal

Inverse-variance weighted NdFeB mean:
  Y-only:   -0.212% +/- 0.026% (8.2 sigma)
  Combined: -0.107% +/- 0.020% (5.3 sigma)

Combining REDUCES significance from 8.2 sigma to 5.3 sigma because:
1. H-plate NdFeB mean is near zero (+0.026%) — pulls combined toward zero
2. A-sample NdFeB mean is POSITIVE (+0.087%) — pulls combined toward zero
3. H/A have fundamentally different systematics (not gain-immune, different geometry)
4. H/A measurements are not as well controlled as Y-plate Helmholtz

The correct approach: present Y, H, A SEPARATELY, not averaged.

## Key Conclusions

1. Y-PLATE DIFFERENTIAL is the headline result: -0.208% +/- 0.028% (7.6 sigma)
   - Gain-immune, 4 materials per plate, randomized, Helmholtz geometry
   - This is the STRONGEST and CLEANEST test

2. H-PLATES provide weak independent evidence:
   - NdFeB-SmCo diff = -0.122% (1.5 sigma) — same direction as Y, but noisy
   - 2.5x more scatter than Y-plates

3. A-SAMPLES are inconclusive:
   - NdFeB-SmCo diff = +0.020% (0.4 sigma) — essentially zero
   - Too few slot-level points (N=13+16) for meaningful statistics

4. NEVER combine Y+H+A into a single average:
   - H/A have different systematics (not gain-immune)
   - H/A means are near zero, diluting the real Y signal
   - Combining drops significance from 8.2 sigma to 5.3 sigma

5. Temperature correction does not change the conclusion:
   - Signal exists at >5 sigma across the entire 23-25 deg C range
   - Per-date correction is data-driven and physically sensible
