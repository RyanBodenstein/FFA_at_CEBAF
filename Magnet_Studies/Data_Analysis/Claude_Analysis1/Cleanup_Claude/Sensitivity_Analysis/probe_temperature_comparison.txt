SECTION 8: CONCLUSIONS AND RECOMMENDATIONS
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------

1. ALL sample types (Y, H, A) used the SAME Teslameter probe at any given time.
   Within-date Y-H differences of +/-0.9C are NORMAL measurement-order effects.

2. The probe was replaced before tunnel deployment (sometime between Dec 2024
   and Jul 2025). The old probe may have read high, but the evidence from the
   ONLY two same-day Y+H measurements (Sep 30 and Nov 20) shows:
     - Sep 30: Y-H = +0.87C (Y measured 1.3h later)
     - Nov 20: Y-H = +0.77C (Y measured ~1h later)
   These are consistent with time-of-day warming, not a +2C probe bias.

3. The comparison of Nov Y (25.0C mean) to Nov H (24.0C mean) on DIFFERENT days
   shows ~1.0C offset, but this includes natural day-to-day variation.

4. The tunnel-era data (NEW probe) shows Y reads 0.0-0.3C LOWER than H,
   consistent with Y being measured first (cooler lab in morning).

5. CRITICAL QUESTION: What external evidence exists that the old probe read
   ~2C high? If this came from comparing Nov 5 Y=25.1C to an independent
   room thermometer reading 22-23C, that would be strong evidence. But if it
   only comes from comparing Y to H/A on different dates, the 2C estimate
   may be an overestimate.

6. IMPACT ON RESULTS:
   - Current correction: subtracts ~2C from pre-deployment Y temps
   - If actual bias was ~1C (consistent with same-day evidence), the
     temperature correction is OVER-applied by ~1C, which would shift
     the differential by ~+0.06% (less correction = more negative result).
   - This is within the stated +/-0.06% temp uncertainty already in the
     error budget, so the overall conclusion is robust.

7. RECOMMENDATION: Investigate whether there is an independent room
   thermometer record for Nov 2024 and the pre-deployment period. This
   would definitively resolve the probe bias magnitude.
