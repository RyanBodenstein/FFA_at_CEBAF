# Probe Bias Assessment — What Is the Correct Pre-Deployment Temperature?
# Date: 2026-03-31 (Task 21f)

## The Original Assumption Was Wrong

We assumed the Y-plate Teslameter probe was reading ~2°C high, based on comparing
Y-plate temps (~24.5-25.4°C) to an H/A Teslameter reading of 23.08°C on Dec 4.

BUT: There is only ONE Teslameter. Y, H, and A samples all use the SAME device
with the SAME probe. The "H/A Teslameter" and "Y-plate Teslameter" are the same
instrument. The probe was replaced between Dec 2024 and Jul 2025.

## What The Same-Day Data Shows

Only 2 pre-deployment dates have both Y and H/A readings:
  - Sep 30, 2024: Y=26.66°C, H=25.80°C → Y-H = +0.87°C (Y measured 1.3h later)
  - Nov 20, 2024: Y=25.38°C, H=24.62°C → Y-H = +0.77°C (Y measured ~1h later)

The Y-H difference is only ~0.8°C, and it's largely explainable by measurement
timing (Y measured later → room warmer).

In the tunnel era (new probe), near-simultaneous Y-H is -0.15°C ± 0.14°C,
confirming that same-probe, same-date differences are small when measured
at the same time.

## The Day-to-Day Lab Temperature Variation Is LARGE

November H-plate readings span 23.0-26.3°C across different days.
The lab temperature varies by 3+ degrees day to day.
Comparing Y on Nov 5 to H on Nov 11 (different days) does NOT measure probe bias.

## Best Estimate of True Lab Temperature During Nov 5/7 Y-Plate Baselines

The only fair comparison for Nov 5/7 Y-plate readings is:
1. Nov 20 same-day: Y=25.4°C, H=24.6°C → Y-H = +0.8°C (includes time-of-day)
2. Pooled Nov Y mean: 24.9°C
3. Pooled Nov H mean (all Nov H dates): 24.0°C → Y-H = +1.0°C (different days)
4. Tunnel-era same-date same-time Y-H: ~-0.15°C (probe reads same, new probe)

If the old probe had NO bias, Nov 5/7 Y temps of 25.1/24.6°C would just be the
actual lab temperature on those days. This is plausible given H=26.3°C on Nov 18.

If the old probe had +0.8°C bias (consistent with same-day Nov 20 data), then:
  Nov 5 actual temp: 25.1 - 0.8 = 24.3°C
  Nov 7 actual temp: 24.6 - 0.8 = 23.8°C

If the old probe had +1.0°C bias (consistent with pooled Nov Y vs H):
  Nov 5 actual temp: 25.1 - 1.0 = 24.1°C
  Nov 7 actual temp: 24.6 - 1.0 = 23.6°C

## Recommendation

The current 23.0°C correction appears to OVERCORRECT by ~1°C.

Best estimate: the true pre-deployment lab temperature was ~24.0 ± 0.5°C
(based on pooled Nov H mean of 24.0°C and same-day Y-H of +0.8°C).

This gives a differential of approximately:
  -0.20% ± 0.03%(stat) ± 0.03%(temp) = -0.20% ± 0.04% (~5-8σ range)

The 23.0°C estimate was based on comparing to Dec 4 H=22.8°C, which was simply
a cold day in the lab — not representative of November conditions.
