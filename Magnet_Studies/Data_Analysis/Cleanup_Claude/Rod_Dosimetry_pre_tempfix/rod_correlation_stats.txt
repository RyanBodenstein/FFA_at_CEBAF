KIRSTEN'S PHOTON DOSE METHODOLOGY (from decipher_guide.txt):
  All units: krad (rods) or krem (converted from OSL mrem ÷ 1e6)
  For photons Q=1, so 1 krad = 1 krem = 1000 rem

  Decision tree per swap period:
  1. Low@600 < 2 krad AND OSL NOT saturated → use OSL reading
  2. Low@600 < 2 krad AND OSL IS saturated  → use 1.5 krad (MEASUREMENT GAP)
  3. 2 < Low@600 < 12 krad                  → average 2 Low@600 readings
  4. Low@600 > 12 AND High@600 > 12         → average 2 High@600 readings
  5. Higher doses → progressively include Low@656, High@600, High@656
  6. Very high doses → average Low@656(2) + High@656(2)

  Key: she uses the BEST available reading at each dose level.
  The 1.5 krad gap value (case 2) is important — it's the interpolated
  estimate for periods where OSL saturated (>1 krad) but rods are below
  threshold (<2 krad).

NEUTRON APPROACH (ours, not Kirsten's):
  We keep our OSL CR-39 neutron pipeline: measured when valid, lower-bound
  flagged when saturated. Kirsten's approach (1 krem for saturated neutrons)
  is noted but not adopted. Future NDX data will supplement neutron doses.
